Ethnic groups by gender

This service measures how experiences and outcomes differ for people of various ethnicities across public sector services.

Gender can have an impact on people’s experiences of public services. For this reason, the service will often analyse data both by ethnicity and gender.

To put the differences in context, it’s also important to understand how gender breaks down within ethnic groups across England and Wales.

Most ethnic groups are made of almost equal numbers of women and men.

However, there are some exceptions. The Other White, Chinese and Black Caribbean groups all contain a higher percentage of women, at 53%.

The Arab and Any Other ethnic groups contain a higher percentage of men, at 58% and 54% respectively.
